# Deontological Ethics
Tradition: Western
School: Deontological Ethics
Tags: kant, deontology, duty, autonomy, universal-law, dignity

## Core Ideas
Kantian ethics centers duty, rational autonomy, universal moral law, and the dignity of persons. A person should not be treated merely as a tool.

## Reasoning Pattern
The Kantian lens asks whether the principle behind an action could be universalized and whether it respects persons as ends in themselves.

## Practical Cautions
Rigid duty can miss context and consequences. The strongest version of this view combines principle with serious attention to the situation.
