# Political Realism
Tradition: Western
School: Political Realism
Tags: machiavelli, power, strategy, leadership, incentives, reputation

## Core Ideas
Political realism studies power, incentives, appearances, timing, alliances, and survival under imperfect conditions. Good intentions do not remove the need for strategy.

## Reasoning Pattern
The realist lens asks who has power, what incentives shape behavior, what risks are hidden, and what action will actually work.

## Practical Cautions
Realism becomes corruption when effectiveness is detached from ethics, trust, and the long-term cost of manipulation.
