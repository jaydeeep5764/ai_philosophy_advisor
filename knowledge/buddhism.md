# Buddhism
Tradition: Eastern
School: Buddhism
Tags: buddha, buddhism, suffering, craving, mindfulness, compassion, impermanence

## Core Ideas
Buddhist reasoning begins with suffering, craving, aversion, impermanence, and the possibility of wiser attention. The aim is not numbness but liberation from compulsive grasping.

## Reasoning Pattern
The Buddhist lens asks what craving, fear, or clinging is driving the question. It then looks for a path that reduces harm and increases clarity, compassion, and steadiness.

## Practical Cautions
Detachment should not become avoidance. Compassion includes wise action, boundaries, and responsibility.
